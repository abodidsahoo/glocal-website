Careers

We are a small, ambitious, internationally focused film production company. We look for people who care deeply about stories — and who want to help bring them to the world.

Post-Production Intern

Open Now April – June 2025 Post-Production Remote / Hybrid, London

We are looking for a motivated and detail-oriented Post-Production Intern to join the team during the post-production phase of Baghuni – Dance Like a Tiger. This is a hands-on, immersive opportunity to work alongside experienced professionals on an internationally recognised feature film.

Duration: April to June 2025 (approximately 10–12 weeks)
Commitment: Part-time or full-time availability considered
Location: Primarily remote, with possible in-person sessions in London

What You'll Be Involved In

  • Supporting the editing and assembly of footage from the Odisha shoot
  • Assisting with colour grading and visual effects coordination
  • Working with the sound design team on audio synchronisation and mix prep
  • Supporting the delivery of assets for international festival submissions
  • Helping coordinate communications between the UK and India-based production teams

Who We Are Looking For

  • Students or recent graduates in Film, Media Production, or a related field
  • Familiarity with editing software such as Adobe Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or Final Cut Pro
  • Strong attention to detail and an organised approach to workflow
  • A genuine interest in international and regional cinema
  • Excellent written and spoken English; knowledge of Odia or Hindi a bonus

If you are based in the UK, USA, or are already embedded in global or regional media — we would love to hear from you. We especially welcome applications from people with a connection to Odia or broader Indian culture who want to contribute to a landmark project.

To apply, send your CV and a short note (no more than 200 words) on why you would like to be involved to:

Apply: partha@glocalfilmsuk.com

Working with Glocal Films UK

We are a production company at an exciting early stage — small enough for your contribution to matter enormously, and connected enough that your work will be seen internationally. Our current production has already been unveiled at Cannes, and we are building towards a global festival and distribution strategy for 2025–2026.

We believe in nurturing talent from within communities that have historically been underrepresented in the global film industry. Whether you are from Odisha, from the Indian diaspora in the UK, or simply someone who believes in the power of culturally authentic storytelling — there may be a place for you here.

We will post future opportunities on this page. If you would like to register your interest for upcoming roles in areas such as production, press & media, festival strategy, or distribution, please write to us at partha@glocalfilmsuk.com.
